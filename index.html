Instructions and Diagrams for the origami animals.

Step by step diagrams are proably the most popular and easiest to follow way to show how to fold things out of paper. Trying to find good origami instructions on the internet can be a lot of work though. To help your search we've put together the largest database of free origami diagrams anywhere on the internet.

Origami Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Origami Chameleon

  1. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.
  2. Their hands and feet have large toes that help them cling to branches.
  3. chameleons can change their skin color to blend into their surroundings for offensive and defensive plays.

Origami Cicada

  1. Cicadas inundate forests as a survival mechanism.
  2. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.

Origami Panda

  1. Their back and white fur are great camouflage for their environment.
  2. Pandas love to eat bamboo.

Origami Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are amazingly complex and intelligent birds.
